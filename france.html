france

savor the sights, taste the art, live la vie française.

introduction

France, the French Republic, is a modern nation in Western Europe, known for its rich culture, influential history, and diverse landscapes. It's the largest country in Western Europe and a global center for art, fashion, and cuisine, attracting the most tourists worldwide. Its capital is Paris, and it is a democratic republic with a hexagonal territory that borders multiple countries and two major seas.

top tourist spots



Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron structure in Paris that is among the most famous landmarks in the world. It is also a technological masterpiece in building-construction history. It was designed and built (1887–89) by Gustave Eiffel and named in his honor.

How to get there?

by Metro
  • Bir-Hakeim (Line 6): This is the closest station, offering less than a 10-minute walk to Entrance 1 of the monument.
  • Trocadéro (Line 9): This station is a short walk away, about 15 minutes from the Tower.
  • École Militaire (Line 8): Located approximately 11 minutes from the Eiffel Tower.
by RER
  • Champ de Mars-Tour Eiffel (RER C): This station provides access to the monument, with a walk of about 5 to 7 minutes to Entrance 1.
by Bus
  • Several bus lines stop near the Eiffel Tower, including lines 82, 30, 42, 69, 86, and 72, with most stops being about a 5 to 15-minute walk from the monument.

Best Time to Visit

The Eiffel Tower is never not busy, but fear not — there are times of day that are a little less hectic than others. Going all the way to the top? Expect to wait in line. Taking the stairs – all 674 of them – to the second floor is less popular, meaning shorter waits. The Eiffel Tower is at its busiest during peak season (June–September), especially on weekends. Between October and May, waiting times are shorter, especially mid-week before 10:30 AM and after 5 PM.





Louvre Museum

As one of the largest, oldest, and grandest museums in the world, the Louvre hosts a treasure trove of artworks spanning diverse cultures and periods of history. Marvel at the museum’s greatest hits: the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, Winged Victory, and stunning collections of prized paintings and Greek and Roman statues. It’s not possible to see all of the museum in one visit, so be inspired to return for your next trip to Paris. Buy timed-entry tickets online to skip the lines as the museum is usually packed. You can also join a guided tour to enjoy a curated experience organized and led by an expert.

Best Time to Visit

The Louvre is Paris’s most-visited attraction, so it pays to be clued up when it comes to how and when to visit. The museum is open from 9AM-6PM daily except Tuesdays (closed) and Fridays, when it stays open until 9.45PM. You might think getting there early is the key to beating the crowds, but you’d be wrong. Morning lines can be brutal and the courtyard is usually packed with impatient tourists well before the doors open. So, if you want to dodge the dreaded Louvre queues, pitch up later in the day. Around 3PM is prime time. By now, the queues will have subsided significantly, and you’ll still have plenty of time left to ogle the art inside.



local foods

bites worth traveling for.

  • French Baguette: No trip to France is complete without sinking your teeth into a crunchy baguette. UNESCO-recognized.
  • Flaky Croissants: Start your morning with buttery, flaky croissants.
  • Moules Frites: Steaming mussels served with fries.
  • Galettes: Buckwheat savory crêpes traditional in Brittany.
  • French Onion Soup: Caramelized onions, melted cheese, and toasted baguette.

traditions

from old tales to living customs — explore them all.

  • Never take wine to a dinner party: The host has chosen the wine; bring chocolates or Cognac instead.
  • Try and arrive at least 15–20 minutes late: It's common for guests to arrive slightly late for dinner to avoid interrupting preparations.
  • Kiss, kiss: Double-cheek kisses are common; in some regions people exchange more than two.
  • Always say hello and goodbye: Greet shopkeepers and those you interact with.
  • You'll have to ask for ice: Drinks are served at room temperature unless you request ice.

events

these will spark joy and connection.

  • Monte Carlo Motor Rally: For more than 90 years, the Monte Carlo Motor Rally has been among the most important rallying events in the world. Held in January, this rally is famed for its treacherous conditions, with much of the route affected by snow and ice at this time of year. Taking place along the French Riviera, it marks the first stage of the larger World Rally Championship.
  • Cannes Film Festival: Every May, the city of Cannes becomes the focal point of the world’s media as it hosts the annual Cannes Film Festival. Celebrities, A-list actors, royalty, and politicians gather on the French Riviera to attend exclusive premieres. While admission is invite-only, the atmosphere across Cannes is electric, with glamorous off-shoot events open to the public and endless people-watching opportunities.
  • Nice Carnival: The largest carnival celebration in France takes place in the Mediterranean city of Nice. Said to rival Rio’s Carnival, this vibrant festival occurs between February and March, lasting around two weeks. It features spectacular float parades, giant sculptures, music, and the famous “Battle of the Flowers,” when thousands of colorful blooms are tossed into the crowd.
  • Tour de France: The world’s most prestigious cycling race covers almost 3,500 kilometers and showcases France’s diverse landscapes. Every July, elite cyclists race through mountains, countryside, and cities, culminating in an iconic finish at the Champs-Élysées in Paris — a global sporting spectacle drawing millions of viewers.
  • Bastille Day: France’s most important national holiday, Bastille Day celebrates the French Revolution and the birth of modern France. Festivities include parades, fireworks, and parties across the nation, with the largest celebration in Paris. The night before, many neighborhoods host lively street dances and communal feasts that continue well past midnight.

travel tips

little tips that make a big difference on the road.

  • Language: Learn basic French greetings — locals appreciate the effort.
  • Tickets: Buy museum or train tickets online to skip queues.
  • Timing: Visit attractions early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds.
  • Transport: The Paris Metro is reliable — get a multi-day pass for unlimited rides.
  • Seasons: Spring (April–June) and autumn (Sept–Oct) offer mild weather and fewer tourists.
